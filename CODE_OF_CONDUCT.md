# Code of Conduct

## Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

pySTAMPS follows a Contributor Covenant-based code of conduct for all project spaces: issues, pull requests, discussions, and code review.

## Our Pledge

We pledge to provide a harassment-free experience for everyone regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, experience level, nationality, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We do not tolerate behavior that excludes, humiliates, or threatens others.

## Our Standards

Examples of positive behavior:

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Respect differing viewpoints and expertise levels.
- Focus on constructive and reproducible technical collaboration.
- Provide clear, actionable feedback.
- Credit authors and contributors appropriately.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Personal attacks, derogatory comments, or harassment.
- Discriminatory language or jokes at any contributor's expense.
- Public/private humiliation or shaming.
- Repeatedly dismissing or ignoring others.
- Sharing private information without permission.

## Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for enforcing this code of conduct and clarifying expected behavior.

## Enforcement

Contributors who experience or witness conduct issues should open an issue with details of the incident and the context.

Any report will be reviewed in good faith and handled with confidentiality and care.

If a behavior is severe, repeated, or unsafe, maintainers may remove contribution privileges or block participation in project channels.
